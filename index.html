The Rule of 72

Yup, it applies to software too.

The Rule of 72

If you have X% growth over one period of time, you'll double your size in 72/X time periods.

Example

If you're growing 12% per month, you'll double your users in roughly 6 months (72/12)

Doubling every six months is a big deal

And it’s extremely stressful

Growing quickly gives you all sorts of new things to think about

  • Covering costs
  • Continuing growth
  • User feedback (email, Twitter, Facebook, etc)
  • Lawyers and stuff
  • Hiring

And those are just the business concerns.

As for the technology side...

You will constantly struggle with

  • Adding features quickly
  • Scope creep
  • Adding the right features (user research)
  • Keeping pages lean and performant
  • Maintaining existing codebase (fixing things that break)

Not to mention

  • Keeping your servers running
  • Coordinating a team
  • Shipping

Today we’re going to learn how to do all those things.

1. Managing your codebase

As a developer, your code is your most important asset

#1 tip for a healthy codebase:

Use version control

Version control stores every change you make to your codebase.

If you (or someone else) changes something bad, it’s almost painless, because you can just jump back to the original.

A simple tutorial on Git?

#2 tip for a healthy codebase:

Have Standards. Stick to them.

What the standards are isn't as vital.

You can just search for "Ruby Coding Standards" and season to taste

Tip #3:

Lint your code

Linting catches small bugs, and also enforces your code standards.

And what good are your standards if they aren't enforced?
